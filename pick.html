Pick Your Instrument!

Violin

The violin is a very famous instrument that was first developed hundreds of years ago. It is a string instrument that is typically played with a bow. It's uses range from baroque music from the 1400s to modern day electric instruments. Although, classical music is superior.

Piano

The piano is an acoustic, stringed musical instrument invented in Italy around the year 1700, in which the strings are struck by hammers. The most famous composers for this instrument includes Frederic Chopin, Ling Ling and Beethoven.

Cello

The cello another instrument in the violin-family. It is a bow-instrument that has a deep and low sound that usually gets to play the lower parts in an orchestra. But there is solo repertoire, for example Bach's Sonata No. 2 for solo cello.

Viola

The viola is very similar to the violin. Often leading to confusion among non-musicians. The viola is played almost exactly like the violin. It's tuned a fifth below. To read sheet music for the viola, you need to learn the C-clef. It's shit.

Erhu

Although not an eastern classical instrument, the erhu is a traditional chinese instrument. It is a two-stringed instrument that is played with a bow, similar to the violin. Thus it's sometimes wrongfully called the "Eastern Fiddle".

Harp

The harp is a very old instrument. Instruments resembling the modern harp were first made thousands of years ago. It is a string instrument that is played by plucking the strings. It is similar to the piano in that each individual string typically only plays one note, it never changes length.
